Computational Affective and Social Cognition Lab

Welcome to our study and thank you for participating! In this study, we are interested in how people infer emotions of others based on other people's experiences. This task will take approximately 30 minutes.

Please ensure that the sound on your computer is turned on.

Do not do this task on a mobile device or tablet.

Legal information: No information pertaining to your personal identity will be collected. Moreover, questions on your demographics (e.g. race, age etc.) collected at the end are optional. At any time during the course of the study, you may withdraw and all provided data will be discarded and not be used by our research team. Your participation in this research is entirely voluntary. if you like to understand more on the the study before participating, you can contact Gerard Yeo at e0545159@u.nus.edu.sg.

Participant Information

The following states some information and frequently asked questions (FAQs) about the study. Please take the time to read if you have any concerns about the study.

Project Title: Modelling Appraisals and Emotions in Narratives

Purpose of project: This study investigates how people interpret the situation that other people are experiencing and also, reason about the emotions felt by other people in the situation.

Who can participate in this project? What is the expected duration of participation?

Anyone who are at least 21 years old are eligible to participate in this study that lasts about 30 minutes.

What is the approximate number of research participants involved?

Approximately 2000 participants will be involved in this study.

What will be done if I take part in this research study?

You will be presented with short videos and tasked to answer questions relating to the video. At the end of the task, there will be a demographic (age, gender, ethnicity, etc.) questionnaire to be filled up (optional).

How will my privacy and the confidentiality of my research records be protected?

All project records and data will be kept strictly confidential. No identifiable personal information (names, identity card or address, etc.) will be collected in this study. All data collected will be kept in accordance to the Universityâ€™s Research Data Management Policy. Research data used in any publication will be kept for a minimum of 10 years before being discarded.

What are the possible discomforts and/or risks for participants?

No risks, discomforts or injury are expected in your participation in this experiment.

What is the compensation for any injury?

There will be no compensation for any injury. However, you can contact the Co-Investigator, Gerard Yeo, if you require assistance in the event of any research-related injury.

Will there be reimbursement for participation?

You will receive US$4 for participation.

What are the possible benefits of this project?

There is no direct benefit to you by participating in this research study. The knowledge gained may benefit the public in the future in terms of understanding how people reason about emotions.

Can I withdraw in the midst of participating in the study?

Yes, you can. Your decision to participate in this project is voluntary and completely up to you. Should you choose to participate, you can still withdraw from the project at any time, without giving any reasons, by informing the principal investigator. All data and/or samples that you have provided will then be discarded.

Whom should I call if I have any questions or problems?

Please contact the Co-Investigator, Gerard Yeo at e0545159@u.nus.edu for all matters related to this project and in the event of project-related problems. For an independent opinion specifically regarding the rights and welfare of research participants, you may contact a staff member of the National University of Singapore Institutional Review Board at telephone (+65) 6516 1234 (Mondays to Thursdays from 8.30am to 6pm, and Fridays from 8.30am to 5.30pm, except public holidays) or email at irb@nus.edu.sg.
